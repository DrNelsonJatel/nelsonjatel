Most people choose a network-analysis tool the way they choose a restaurant on
holiday: by the photos. That is how you end up with a beautiful graph that
answers a question nobody asked. In this short I line up three of the tools I
reach for most, and the honest trade I make with each.

The point is not which software is best. It is matching the tool to the
question. A quick visual gut-check, a defensible centrality measure, and a
publication-ready figure are three different jobs, and the tool that wins one
often loses the others.
