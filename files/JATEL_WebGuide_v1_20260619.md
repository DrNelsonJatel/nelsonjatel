## 1. Positioning (the thesis)

Nelson's distinctive position, the thing almost no one else can claim, is the **intersection of water governance and social network analysis (SNA)**. That intersection is the throughline that unifies the speaking work, the video series, and the books, and it is grounded in his doctoral research (SNA of a 50-year water-governance meeting network).

The site should lead with that intersection, then let the **Speaking** page broaden into governance and networks generally (boards, AGMs, leadership teams), since that is where bookings come from. Do not open with generic "corporate governance trends"; that field is crowded and undifferentiated. Open with the specific, defensible angle and earn the broader work from there.

**One-line positioning (use on hero / meta):**
> I help leaders see the hidden networks behind their decisions, in water, in governance, and in any organisation that runs on relationships.

## 7. Design tokens: colour

Carry the locked end-card system (already tested for legibility).

| Token | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| `ground-deep` | `#08202B` | darkest ground, hero top, CTA text on amber |
| `ground` | `#0E2A38` | primary background |
| `ground-soft` | `#0C2E3D` | section background variation |
| `ink` | `#EAF2F4` | primary text on dark (body, headlines) |
| `slate` | `#7FA0AB` | secondary text, captions |
| `recessive` | `#5E7A84` | disclaimer, fine print |
| `node` | `#4A6E7C` | motif nodes, subtle network texture |
| `signal` | `#F3B24E` | the accent. CTAs, the traced line, key emphasis only |
| `signal-hi` | `#FFD27A` | signal highlight / hover |
| `hairline` | `#26424C` | dividers, faint rules |

**Contrast rules:** body text is `ink` on dark, never amber. `signal` is for large display, the motif, and CTA fills only. CTA buttons: `signal` fill with `ground-deep` text (high contrast). Verify every text/background pair against **WCAG AA** before launch; flag any that fail.

## 8. Design tokens: type, layout, signature, motion

**Typeface roles** (all available; cohesive with the end-card):
- **Display (H1–H2):** *Instrument Serif*, editorial, warm, and distinctive. Use with restraint at large sizes.
- **Body:** *Instrument Sans*, clean, pairs with the serif, strong legibility for paragraphs and UI.
- **Utility (eyebrows, labels, captions, credentials, data):** *IBM Plex Mono*, the analytical, instrument-panel voice.

**Type scale (suggested, rem):** H1 3.5 / H2 2.25 / H3 1.5 / body 1.0625 / caption 0.8125. Generous line-height on body (1.6). Mono eyebrows in small caps / letter-spaced.

**Layout:** editorial and spacious. Treat negative space as volume (water), not emptiness. Grid-based, mobile-first (the shorts audience is on phones). Eyebrow labels (mono) should encode something true (section type), not decorate. Avoid `01 / 02 / 03` numbering unless the content is genuinely sequential.

**Signature element (spend the boldness here, keep everything else quiet):** the **traced signal**, a single amber line that resolves out of a cool, scattered node-field, exactly as on the end-card. Use it as: the hero graphic (with a draw-on animation), and as section dividers (a thin amber hairline that "traces" between sections). Do not repeat the full motif everywhere; one strong hero instance plus the hairline echo.

**Imagery:** deep-water tones; real network-graph visuals; professional stage photography. Apply a teal duotone to photos to unify. No generic stock, no clip-art "handshake/network" clichés.

**Motion:** restrained. Hero signal-line draw-on; subtle scroll-reveals; hover micro-interactions on cards/CTAs. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Extra animation reads as templated; less is more.
